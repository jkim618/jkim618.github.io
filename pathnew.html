Context
USI PATH is a data-driven platform that helps organizations identify and mitigate top business risks using curated vendor solutions. I led the UX redesign of its most critical feature, the Solution Matrix, to improve usability and information hierarchy.
Problem
PATH began as a promising idea with only a few rough stakeholder mockups built in PowerPoint. The original experience was cluttered, inconsistent, and lacked any clear structure. My challenge was to turn that vague concept into a usable, scalable platform producers could rely on daily.
Impact
Since launch, PATH has grown from a rough concept into a fully operational platform with measurable business impact. The redesign led to a 33.61% producer adoption rate across USI regions, a 69.27% increase in unique users, and a 31.77% increase in total platform sessions‚Äîdemonstrating strong engagement and widespread internal adoption.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

How might we design a more intuitive platform that enables users to confidently navigate, evaluate, and act on risk control strategies?

SYNTHESIS

Key Insights from Research

Stakeholder interviews provided crucial insights that helped align the redesign with both business objectives and user needs, leading to a clear summary of key goals.

The platform needs customization based on industry and company size.

Solution Matrix

HEURISTIC EVALUATION

Major Usability Issues in the Initial Design

Heuristics #4 & #6

Ambiguous Labels & Filter Proximity

The unclear checkbox labels and the close proximity of vertical and subvertical filters create confusion about what is being selected and filtered.

Heuristics #5 & #7

Interdependent Dropdown Confusion

The relationship between the two dropdowns is not clearly communicated, leading to potential confusion about how one selection impacts the other.

Heuristics #8

Cluttered Layout & Overload

The crowded two-column layout and excessive checkboxes overwhelm users, making it harder to focus and navigate the interface.

FINAL DESIGNS

Improving Data Navigation

The initial design relied solely on dropdowns for data selection, which was confusing and lacked a clear hierarchy. To address these limitations, I implemented a dual approach: a top-level filter using dropdowns and the introduction of navigation tabs.

Before: Overloaded Dropdown UX

  • ‚úó No Clear Hierarchy: Stacked vertical + subvertical dropdowns made it hard to understand what controlled what.
  • ‚úó Linked Filters = Confusion: Dropdowns were auto-linked, causing unpredictable changes and frustration.
  • ‚úó Overwhelming Layout: Too many modules shown at once created cognitive overload.

Final Design: Filter Clarity + Streamlined Hierarchy

  • ‚úì Strong Layout Separation: Header filters are visually separated from the main content, improving scanability and reducing cognitive load.
  • ‚úì Top-Level Industry Context: A dedicated header allows users to set industry and subvertical filters, with subverticals dynamically updating based on the selected industry.
  • ‚úì Line of Coverage Controls Content: The ‚ÄúLine of Coverage‚Äù dropdown clearly governs the content below, reinforcing hierarchy and purpose.
