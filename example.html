Stakeholder Discovery

At the start of the project, I conducted discovery sessions with underwriters, business analysts, and product managers to understand the limitations of the current system and define requirements for a manual override tool.

During these sessions, I explored questions such as:

  • How are carrier recommendations currently managed?
  • In what situations would you need to promote or exclude a carrier?
  • What level of control is necessary â€” regional, state, or industry-specific?
  • How important is visibility and transparency when manual adjustments are made?

Key Insights:

  • No Manual Control: Users had no ability to adjust backend-driven recommendations, creating gaps between strategic goals and system outputs.
  • Granular Adjustments Needed: Stakeholders emphasized the need for state-level and NAICS code-level targeting rather than broad categories.
  • Transparency was Critical: Manual changes needed to be clearly visible and auditable to maintain user trust in the system.
